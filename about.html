Founded in 1959 by Berry Gordy Jr., Motown Records is one of the most influential forces in music from the 20th century. As the first African American owned music label, Motown led the way for the racial integration of popular music. Known for the iconic “Motown Sound” artists and songwriters crafted songs to appeal to the masses. Based out of Detroit, Michigan, the Hitsville Studios churned out chart topping songs with their factory-like production process. Motown was the most successful soul music label, with a net worth of $61 million dollars. During the 1960s, Motown achieved 79 records in the top-ten of the Billboard Hot 100.
‍
The Motown Sound has had a profound impact on the history of contemporary music. From boy bands to trap music, Motown’s influence is all around us. It is clear that popular music has its roots in Black artistry. “The Motown Influence” serves to celebrate these roots and share the joy of Motown music.
